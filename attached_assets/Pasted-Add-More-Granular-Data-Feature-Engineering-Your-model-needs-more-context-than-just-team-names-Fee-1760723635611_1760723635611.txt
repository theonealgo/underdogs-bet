Add More Granular Data (Feature Engineering)
Your model needs more context than just team names. Feed it specific, predictive stats for each game. Consider adding:

Advanced Metrics: These are often more predictive than basic stats.

DVOA (Defense-adjusted Value over Average): Measures a team's efficiency on a play-by-play basis, adjusted for the opponent.

EPA (Expected Points Added): Measures how many points a team gains or loses on any given play compared to the league average.

Player-Specific Data:

Quarterback Performance: Include the starting QB's stats like QBR, passer rating, or ANY/A (Adjusted Net Yards per Attempt).

Key Injuries: Create a variable that accounts for injuries to star players (e.g., a score representing the value of injured players).

Situational Factors:

Rest Disparity: Days of rest since the last game (especially important for Thursday games after a Sunday game).

Travel: Distance traveled and time zones crossed.

Bye Week: Whether a team is coming off a bye week.

Recent Form:

Performance over the last 3-4 games can be more indicative than season-long averages. Include moving averages for points scored, yards allowed, turnover differential, etc.

2. Tune Your XGBoost Model 🛠️
Out-of-the-box models are rarely optimized. You can significantly improve XGBoost's performance by tuning its hyperparameters. This involves experimenting with settings to find the combination that works best on your historical data. Key parameters to tune include:

n_estimators: The number of trees in the model.

max_depth: The maximum depth of each tree.

learning_rate: How much each new tree corrects the previous ones.

3. Create a Smarter Ensemble
Instead of just picking one model, you can combine them intelligently. Your "Main Pick" is already an ensemble. To improve it:

Weighted Averaging: Don't give each model an equal vote. Give more weight to the model that has proven more accurate in backtesting. For example, your final probability might be (0.85 * XGB_Prob) + (0.15 * Elo_Prob).

Add More Models: You could train other types of models (like a Logistic Regression or a LightGBM model) on your data and add their "votes" into the final prediction, again weighting them based on performance.
