That's a very common experience in predictive modeling, especially in sports! Elo and a basic XGBoost are great starting points, but moving from a 50-60% accuracy to consistently higher levels requires deeper feature engineering and a more sophisticated modeling approach.
Here is a multi-step plan to significantly improve the accuracy of your models:


Phase 1: Advanced Feature Engineering (The Biggest Lever)

The most effective way to improve your XGBoost model (and thus your overall pick) is to give it better data. You need features that capture recent performance, match-up dynamics, and true team strength more accurately than simple season-long averages.

1. Deeper Elo Features

Instead of just one Elo rating, create several to capture different aspects of team strength:
* Weighted/Time-Decay Elo: Apply a decay factor so that recent games impact the rating much more than games from the beginning of the season.
    * Feature: Home_Team_Elo_Decayed, Away_Team_Elo_Decayed
* Offense/Defense Split Elo: Calculate a separate Elo rating for a team's Offensive strength (based on points scored vs. expectation) and Defensive strength (based on points allowed vs. expectation).
    * Feature: Home_Off_Elo, Away_Def_Elo, Off_Def_Matchup_Delta (e.g., Home_Off_Elo - Away_Def_Elo)

2. Form and Momentum Features

Recent performance is critical in a long season:
* Rolling Averages (Past 3-5 Games):
    * Home_Team_Avg_Point_Differential_L5 (Last 5 games)
    * Home_Team_Avg_Turnover_Margin_L3
    * Home_Team_Win_Pct_L8 (Last 8 games)
* Against-The-Spread (ATS) Performance: How has the team performed relative to market expectations recently? This is a great feature as it measures team performance against a line set by experts.
    * Home_Team_ATS_Cover_Pct_L5

3. Matchup-Specific Features

How does a team perform against the style of its opponent?
* Rushing/Passing Efficiency Differential: Calculate a team's rush/pass DVOA (or a simple metric of yards per play) and compare it directly to the opponent's rush/pass DVOA allowed.
    * Example: Home_Run_Off_vs_Away_Run_Def_Delta
* Venue/Time-of-Day Features (Categorical): Does a West Coast team perform poorly in an early Sunday game on the East Coast?
    * Feature: Timezone_Travel_Delta, Dome_vs_Outdoor


Phase 2: Advanced Modeling Techniques

Your XGBoost is already strong, but you can build a more robust ensemble system.

4. Stacking / Ensemble Modeling

Instead of just picking the model with the highest probability, use a second machine learning model (like a simple Logistic Regression or a small Neural Network) to learn how to weight or combine the output of your base models.
1. Base Models (Level 0): Your individual models.
    * XGB_Win_Prob
    * Elo_Win_Prob
2. Meta-Model (Level 1): Train a new model (e.g., a simple Linear Regression or Logistic Regression) where the features are the Level 0 predictions and the target is the actual game outcome.
XGB_Win_Prob	Elo_Win_Prob	Feature_A	...	Actual_Winner
0.679	0.735	0.15	...	1 (PHI wins)
0.235	0.298	-0.05	...	0 (KC loses)
This "Meta-Model" will learn the biases of your base models (e.g., "XGBoost is generally overconfident, so rely on its predictions more when the Elo model is also high").

5. Hyperparameter Tuning and Cross-Validation

You mentioned applying "stuff," but robust tuning is crucial for XGBoost.
* Systematic Search: Use techniques like Grid Search or Randomized Search to find the optimal combination of parameters (learning_rate, max_depth, n_estimators, subsample, etc.).
* Time-Series Cross-Validation: Crucially, you must validate your model in a time-aware manner. You cannot use future data to predict the past.
    * Example: Train on 2018-2023, Test on 2024. Then Train on 2018-2024, Test on 2025 (Weeks 1-7). This prevents "data leakage."


Phase 3: Incorporating External Wisdom

The betting market is a massive, real-time ensemble model of professional opinions. Ignore it at your peril.

6. Betting Market Features

The most powerful features you can add often come from the opening and closing betting lines. The odds already incorporate most public information (injuries, weather, coaching changes) and are considered a highly efficient predictor.
* Features to Add to XGBoost:
    * Opening_Moneyline_Home
    * Closing_Moneyline_Home
    * Opening_Point_Spread
    * Closing_Point_Spread
    * Line_Movement_Delta (e.g., Closing_Spread - Opening_Spread)
If your model is trying to predict the outcome better than the market, these features will help your model understand the market's initial expectation and how it changed due to late-breaking news.
Your goal is now to find the small, persistent edge where your features and modeling technique are consistently more accurate than the implied probability from the betting odds.
